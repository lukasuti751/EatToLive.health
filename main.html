Herbs at a glance

A short reference for common herbs and their traditional uses. Always consult a practitioner for health decisions.

Basil

Antioxidant, anti-inflammatory. Supports digestion and stress relief. Use in teas or fresh in meals.

culinary · digestive

Chamomile

Calming, sleep support. Gentle for stomach comfort. Often taken as tea before bed.

calm · sleep

Echinacea

Traditionally used for immune support. Popular in cold season preparations.

immune

Ginger

Nausea relief, anti-inflammatory. Supports digestion and circulation. Fresh or dried.

digestive · warming

Lavender

Relaxation, sleep, mild mood support. Aromatherapy and culinary use.

calm · aroma

Peppermint

Digestive comfort, clarity. Refreshing in tea or as essential oil.

digestive · cooling

Turmeric

Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant. Often paired with black pepper for absorption.

inflammatory · spice

Thyme

Antimicrobial, respiratory support. Culinary and medicinal use.

respiratory · culinary

Rosemary

Memory and circulation. Antioxidant. Common in cooking and aromatherapy.

cognitive · culinary

Sage

Traditional throat and mouth support. Antioxidant. Culinary sage for seasoning.

throat · antioxidant

Fennel

Digestive comfort, bloating. Seeds and bulb used in cooking and tea.

digestive

Elderberry

Immune support, cold and flu season. Often as syrup or supplement.

immune

Lemon balm

Calming, mild mood support. Gentle for stomach. Tea or tincture.

calm · digestive
